MS DHONI

Mahendra Singh Dhoni, popularly known as MS Dhoni, is one of India’s most celebrated cricketers and a legendary former captain of the Indian national team. Born on July 7, 1981, in Ranchi, Jharkhand, Dhoni is renowned for his calm demeanor, sharp leadership, and exceptional skills as a wicketkeeper-batsman. Under his captaincy, India won major ICC trophies, including the 2007 T20 World Cup, 2011 Cricket World Cup, and 2013 Champions Trophy, making him the only captain to achieve this rare feat. Known as “Captain Cool” Dhoni is admired not only for his cricketing achievements but also for his humility, sportsmanship, and ability to inspire millions around the world.

Early Life

Dhoni was born on 7 July 1981 in Ranchi, Bihar (now in Jharkhand) in a Hindu Rajput family to Pan Singh and Devaki Devi. His parents hailed from Lwali village in Uttar Pradesh (now Uttarakhand) and he was the youngest of three children. His family spells the surname as "Dhauni".The spelling "Dhoni" emerged due to a spelling mistake in his school certificates and, despite repeated attempts by his family, has never been rectified. Dhoni did his schooling from DAV Jawahar Vidya Mandir, where he started playing football as a goalkeeper, but later moved to play cricket on the suggestion of his coach Keshav Banerjee.From 2001 to 2003, Dhoni worked as a Travelling Ticket Examiner (TTE) at Kharagpur under South Eastern Railway zone of Indian Railways.

School Cricket (1995–1998)

MS Dhoni studied at DAV Jawahar Vidya Mandir, Ranchi, where his sporting talent first began to shine. Although he initially played football as a goalkeeper, his coach noticed his quick reflexes and sent him to keep wickets for a local cricket club match. This unexpected opportunity revealed Dhoni’s natural wicketkeeping ability.
From then on, he became the regular wicketkeeper for his school cricket team and participated in various inter-school tournaments. His batting style was aggressive even as a student, often surprising bowlers with powerful strokes. He also played badminton and football, showing his versatility in sports.His consistent performances at the school level helped him get noticed by local cricket clubs, opening the path to district and state-level cricket selections. This school cricket phase laid the foundation for his future success, building his confidence, fitness, and passion for the game.

District & Club Cricket

After proving his talent in school cricket, MS Dhoni began playing for local cricket clubs in Ranchi, where he continued to sharpen his wicketkeeping and batting skills. One of his major breaks came when he joined the Coal Fields Limited (CCL) cricket team. His explosive batting and quick wicketkeeping reflexes made him stand out in local tournaments.
While playing for CCL, Dhoni balanced cricket with a job as a ticket collector at Kharagpur railway station. Despite the work commitments, he never missed practice sessions or matches, showing his dedication to the sport. His consistent performances in club cricket led to his selection for the Bihar U-19 team.
In the 1998–99 Cooch Behar Trophy, Dhoni scored 488 runs in 9 matches, including several match-winning knocks. This success at the district and club level marked the beginning of his rise in professional cricket, opening the door to his Ranji Trophy debut the following year.

State-Level Cricket

MS Dhoni’s impressive performances in school, district, and club cricket earned him a spot in the Bihar U-19 team. In the 1998–99 Cooch Behar Trophy, he scored 488 runs in 9 matches, showcasing his aggressive batting style and sharp wicketkeeping skills. His performances caught the attention of state selectors, leading to his inclusion in the Bihar Ranji Trophy team.
Dhoni made his Ranji Trophy debut just 18 years old during the 1999–2000 season against Assam, where he scored 68 runs in his first match. Over the next few seasons, he continued to play for Bihar in domestic tournaments like the Ranji Trophy and the Deodhar Trophy, developing his reputation as a fearless lower-order batsman and a reliable wicketkeeper.
Even at the state level, Dhoni’s calm temperament, power-hitting ability, and lightning-fast stumpings stood out, laying the groundwork for his selection into the East Zone team and eventually the India A squad, which became his stepping stone to the national team.
